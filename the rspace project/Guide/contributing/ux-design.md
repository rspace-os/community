# UX & Design Contributions

RSpace welcomes contributions from UX designers, UI designers, user researchers, and anyone with an eye for improving research software. Our UX contribution process is still in its early days — here's an honest picture of where things stand and how to get involved.

## Challenge Us on the Current UI

The most valuable thing a UX contributor can do right now is bring **outside eyes to RSpace**. If you're a designer or researcher, we'd love your honest take — heuristic reviews, usability critiques, accessibility observations, or workflow analysis. You don't need a brief from us to get started: [try RSpace's community server](https://community.researchspace.com/) or [run it locally via docker](https://github.com/rspace-os/rspace-docker), explore it as a researcher would, and tell us what you find.

**How to share your findings**:
- Open an issue on [rspace-web](https://github.com/rspace-os/rspace-web/issues) with the label `RDM UX`
- Or reach out at opensource@researchspace.com to discuss before diving in

We review all UX issues and discuss them as a team. There's no formal process beyond that yet — but your input directly shapes what gets prioritised.

## Find an Existing Challenge

We also use the `RDM UX` label for thorny or non-straightforward feature requests — cases where the right UX solution isn't obvious and we'd benefit from a designer's perspective. Browsing these issues is a great way to find something concrete to engage with without having to start from scratch.

→ **[Browse `RDM UX` issues](https://github.com/rspace-os/rspace-web/issues?q=is%3Aissue+is%3Aopen+label%3A%22RDM+UX%22)**

Feel free to comment, ask questions, or propose a direction directly on the issue or in our office hours.

## What We're Looking For

We especially welcome input on:
- **Accessibility** — WCAG compliance, keyboard navigation, screen reader support
- **Onboarding** — first-time user experience for researchers
- **Mobile and responsive behaviour** — key workflows on smaller screens
- **Information architecture** — navigation, naming, how things are organised
- **Workflow critique** — anything that feels unnecessarily complex or confusing

## Tools

We work in **[Penpot](https://penpot.app)** — an open-source design tool that fits well with our open-source ethos. If you'd like to contribute design files or prototypes, Penpot is our preferred format.

Other tools are fine too — Figma, Sketch, even annotated screenshots or paper sketches. We care about the ideas more than the file format.

## Participate in UX Research

We run periodic UX research studies — interviews, usability tests, and other activities — that are open to community participation. Active research tracks and sign-up links are listed in the **[UX Research](../../ux-research/ux-research.md)** section of this repository.

## Get Involved

UX is an area we want to grow. If you're interested in contributing more regularly — user research, design system work, or structured usability testing — reach out and let's have a conversation about what's possible.
