# About the RSpace Project

## What is RSpace?

RSpace is an open-source research data management platform for researchers, research data managers, and research infrastructure providers designed to be a user-friendly and integrated frontend for researchers to efficiently connect and utilize research infrastructure in sustainable FAIR research workflows across the entire research lifecycle.

### Core Capabilities

**Electronic Lab Notebook (ELN)**
Digital workspace for documenting experiments, protocols, and observations with rich media and chemistry support, templates, version control, and full auditability.

**Inventory Management**
Track samples, reagents, and equipment with customizable metadata, locations, and relationships.

**Infrastructure orchestration**
Connect research workflows with tools, services and infrastructure.

**FAIR Data Support**
Research workflows with built-in features and FAIR enabling resources like persistent identifiers to make your research data Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable.

## Our Vision

We envision RSpace as a **user-friendly and integrated frontend for research infrastructure**—a platform that seamlessly orchestrates the diverse services, infrastructure, and digital tools researchers need, reducing friction and enabling sustainable FAIR research workflows.

## Current State

RSpace is production-ready software used by research institutions worldwide. The open-source project is actively developed with:

- Regular releases every 4-5 weeks
- Growing integration ecosystem
- Community office hours every other month
- Enterprise grade services provided by ResearchSpace

## Why Open Source?

As an extension of our collaborative product development model we released RSpace as fully open source in June 2024 to:

- **Foster collaboration**: Enable the research community to shape the platform
- **Increase transparency**: Open development and contribution
- **Extend reach**: Make research data management solutions accessible worldwide
- **Drive innovation**: Encourage community-built integrations and features
- **Support sovereignty**: Give institutions control over their research infrastructure

## The Community
